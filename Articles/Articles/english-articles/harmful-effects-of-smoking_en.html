Bad Effects From Smoking

✍️ Written by Ahmed Trochman | 🗓️ 2 August 2025

Introduction

Smoking is considered one of the most dangerous negative health habits worldwide. It causes numerous diseases that affect both individuals and society. Despite ongoing awareness campaigns, the number of smokers continues to rise.

Major Health Risks of Smoking

  • Increased risk of heart disease and strokes.
  • Weakened respiratory system and higher chances of developing lung cancer.
  • Deterioration of oral health, including bad breath and damaged teeth.
  • Negative impact on skin and overall appearance due to reduced oxygen supply.

Secondhand Smoking

The harm of smoking is not limited to smokers alone—it also affects those around them, especially children and pregnant women. This is known as secondhand smoking, a major cause of respiratory diseases in children.

How to Quit Smoking?

  1. Make a sincere decision to quit.
  2. Seek support from family or friends.
  3. Use nicotine replacement therapies if needed.
  4. Replace the bad habit with healthy alternatives such as exercise.

📌 For more information, visit the World Health Organization – Tobacco page.
